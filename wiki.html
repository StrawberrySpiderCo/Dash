Overview

To understand the network topology of CSV Family Health better, it's crucial to get familiar with some key terms. These terms will give you an idea of how these technologies are interconnected and used within the organization. To make it easier to understand the functionality of each item, I will use a car analogy.

  • Router
  • Switch
  • Firewall
  • WAN
  • LAN
  • Internal traffic
  • External traffic
  • MPLS
  • IPsec
  • SDWAN
  • ISP

Router

The Router is a network device that acts as a driver of the car, directing data packets to the appropriate destination. It can provide control and manage the flow of data in a specific direction. Just as a driver decides which route to take to reach a particular destination, the router also determines the path for data packets to travel. Routers are essential in connecting multiple networks together, such as a LAN and a WAN, enabling them to communicate with each other.

Switch

In contrast, a Switch is a network device that connects multiple devices within a network. It receives data packets and then forwards them to their intended recipient. It's like the transmission of a car that directs the flow of power to the appropriate wheels. Switches can handle heavy traffic and can intelligently segment the network and forward data packets based on their destination.

Firewall

The Firewall is a network security device that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predetermined security rules. It acts as the security system of a car, ensuring that only authorized traffic is allowed to enter the network while blocking any unauthorized access, much like how a car security system prevents unauthorized access and theft of a vehicle. Firewalls can be hardware, software, or a combination of both, just like how a car security system can consist of physical components like locks and alarms, as well as software features like remote locking and GPS tracking. Firewalls are essential in protecting against various cyber threats such as malware, viruses, and hackers, just as a car security system is essential in preventing theft and vandalism of a vehicle.

WAN

The WAN or Wide Area Network is a network that connects devices and networks over a broad geographical area, such as cities or countries. It's like a highway system that enables data to travel across different locations. WANs are typically managed by service providers and are used by businesses and organizations to connect their different offices and remote workers.

LAN

The LAN or Local Area Network, on the other hand, is a network that connects devices within a single location, such as an office or building. It's like the local streets connecting different locations within a city. LANs can be used to share resources such as printers, files, and internet connections.

Internal Traffic

Internal traffic refers to data traffic that occurs within a single location or network, such as within a LAN or a single office building. It's like the traffic within a city that is confined within its boundaries.

External traffic

External traffic that occurs between different locations or networks, such as between different cities or countries. It's like the traffic that moves between different cities.

MPLS

MPLS or Multiprotocol Label Switching is a technique used by service providers to create private networks that provide a direct and secure route between locations. It's like a private toll road that enables data to travel quickly and securely without having to rely on public roads. MPLS is commonly used by businesses and organizations that require secure and reliable connections between their different offices. These MPLS lines do not have an internet connection and just connect one office to another.

IPsec VPN

An IPsec VPN or Internet Protocol Security Virtual Private Network is a type of VPN that allows two or more geographically separated networks to be connected securely over the public Internet. It encrypts and authenticates all traffic between the sites, providing a secure and private connection. This type of VPN is commonly used by organizations that have multiple locations and need to share resources or communicate between sites. It's like a dedicated express lane on the highway system that connects two cities, ensuring that traffic travels securely and efficiently between them.

SDWAN

SDWAN or Software-Defined Wide Area Network is a network architecture that uses software to automate and manage the network. It's like a GPS system that provides the most efficient and reliable route between locations by utilizing multiple WAN technologies and automatically rerouting traffic if a primary route is unavailable. SDWAN is becoming increasingly popular as businesses and organizations look for ways to reduce their network costs while maintaining high levels of performance and security.

ISP

An ISP or Internet Service Provider is a company that provides internet access to customers. It's like the gas that keeps a car going, providing the necessary bandwidth for data to travel across the network. ISPs can be divided into two categories: consumer ISPs, which provide internet access to individuals and small businesses, and business ISPs, which provide internet access to larger organizations with more significant bandwidth requirements.

    • MPLS offers scalability and flexibility, allowing for easy integration with other networking technologies.

    Cons to MPLS

    • Is expensive.
    • Not as widely available as other networking technologies.
    • Is reliant on one company.

    • The other uplink connections that our sites have are called IPsec VPNs. We have 20 of these sites and they are connected through these IPsec tunnels as seen below depicted as blue lines. These tunnels provide a secure and dedicated connection for our internal traffic, separate from the public traffic. The sites with IPsec tunnels have their own internet line, which means that externally-bound data leaves directly from the site instead of going to the Shafter Datacenter but due to the current hub and spoke model sites cannot directly communicate with each other. The blue lines in the diagram represent our dedicated express lanes on the highway, ensuring a secure and efficient communication between our sites.

    Future of the Network

  • SD-WAN in CSV

    • CSV family health already has a couple sites that are using SD-wan and going back to our analogy sd-wan is acting like a gps within our car giving us the best route to a destination based on the traffic and congestion. The future of CSV family health is converting our existing MPLS sites into the IPsec sites and then adding a secondary ISP internet circuit to those sites for added redundancy. This increases network reliablity and stability by enabling a failover circuit if the primary fails. In addition to increased reliability and stability, the adoption of SD-WAN technology also allows for better network visibility and control. SD-WAN provides a centralized management platform, allowing IT teams to easily monitor network performance and security across all sites. This simplifies network management and troubleshooting, reducing the time and resources required to maintain the network. The migration from MPLS to IPsec also brings cost savings, as IPsec is typically less expensive than MPLS. Additionally, IPsec provides greater flexibility and scalability, allowing CSV Family Health to easily add new sites or expand existing ones as needed. The adoption of SD-WAN technology and the transition to IPsec represents a major step forward for CSV Family Health's network infrastructure, providing a more robust, efficient, and cost-effective solution that can better serve its patients and healthcare providers.

    We currrently have no way of monitoring configuration drift which can lead to a range of issues that can have significant negative impacts on network stability, performance, security, and compliance. For instance, a configuration mismatch can cause network devices to function improperly, leading to instability and unpredictable network behavior. This can cause network downtime, loss of productivity, and potentially lost revenue. Configuration mismatch can also result in reduced network performance, as devices may not be able to communicate effectively or efficiently due to inconsistencies in their configurations. Furthermore, configuration mismatch can create security vulnerabilities in the network. Inconsistent or incorrect configurations may leave network devices open to attack, which can lead to data breaches, loss of confidential information, and other security incidents. Finally, many industries and organizations have specific regulatory requirements regarding network configurations. A configuration mismatch can cause non-compliance with these regulations, which can result in penalties, fines, or other legal issues. Therefore, it is crucial for organizations to implement measures to prevent and address configuration drift to avoid these negative impacts on their networks.

    We currently have no monitioring and failure to monitor UPS units can lead to issues such as battery failure, overloading, environmental and maintenance issues, and a lack of visibility, which can impact the reliability and performance of critical equipment, and increase the risk of downtime

    Security

    Most brands we have use the same username and password though convenient to us, when multiple devices in a network share the same username and password for login, it can create significant security issues. Any compromise of that login information would allow an attacker unrestricted access to that entire portion of the network, including sensitive data and critical systems. This setup also makes it difficult to track who has access to what resources, which can complicate access management and lead to potential compliance violations.

    Router

    A router is a network device that forwards data packets between computer networks. It operates at the OSI model's third layer (the network layer) and uses routing tables to direct traffic. Routers can connect LANs, WANs, and the Internet and can perform other functions such as network address translation (NAT) and virtual private network (VPN) connectivity.

    Switch

    A switch is a network device that connects devices together on a local area network (LAN) by using packet switching to forward and filter data to its destination. Switches operate at the OSI model's second layer (the data link layer) and can handle heavy network traffic efficiently.

    Firewall

    A firewall is a network security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predetermined security rules. Firewalls can be hardware, software, or a combination of both and are used to protect against unauthorized access to a network or device. They can also be used to filter network traffic and prevent malware from entering a network.

    WAN

    A Wide Area Network (WAN) is a network that spans a large geographical area, such as a city or country. It connects multiple local area networks (LANs) and can be used to enable communication between different sites or offices. WANs are often managed by service providers and can use various technologies such as MPLS or VPNs to ensure secure and reliable connectivity.

    LAN

    A Local Area Network (LAN) is a network that connects devices within a small area such as an office, building, or campus. It can be used to share resources like printers and files and provide internet access to connected devices. LANs are usually faster and more reliable than wide area networks (WANs).

    Internal Traffic

    Internal traffic refers to data traffic that occurs within a single network, such as within a LAN or a data center. It can be used to transmit data between devices and applications on the same network.

    External Traffic

    External traffic refers to data traffic that occurs between different networks, such as between a LAN and the Internet. It can be used to transmit data between devices and applications on different networks and may require additional security measures like firewalls or VPNs.

    MPLS

    Multiprotocol Label Switching (MPLS) is a a routing technology that uses labels to efficiently forward data packets through a network. Labels are added to each packet as they enter the network and are used to determine the next hop in the network.

    IPsec VPN

    Internet Protocol Security (IPsec) Virtual Private Network (VPN) is a type of VPN that provides secure communication between geographically separated networks over the public Internet. It encrypts and authenticates all traffic between the sites, providing a secure and private connection. The IPsec VPN tunnels are established by setting up a Security Association (SA) between the two endpoints. The SA contains the security parameters used to secure the traffic, such as the encryption algorithm, authentication method, and key exchange algorithm. When a data packet is sent from one endpoint to another, the packet is encrypted and encapsulated within another packet. The encapsulated packet is then sent over the internet to the other endpoint where it is decrypted and the original packet is extracted.

    SDWAN

    Software-Defined Wide Area Network (SDWAN) is a network architecture that uses software to automate and manage the network. SDWAN can be used to improve network performance

    ISP

    An ISP or Internet Service Provider is a company that provides uplink access to customers

    Current status of CSV Health Network

  • MPLS in CSV

    • CSV Family Health's network, there are 19 sites and eMedApps are connected to the MPLS cloud via Lumen, which acts as ISP for the MPLS service. Each site has a router that connects to the MPLS cloud via a Virtual Private Network (VPN) connection. When a site sends data over the network, the data is encapsulated with a label that identifies the destination site. The MPLS cloud then uses this label to determine the next hop in the network, effectively creating a virtual path through the network for the data. All data sent over the MPLS cloud is directed to CSV Family Health's Datacenter in Shafter. The Datacenter acts as a hub for the network, where it determines whether the data needs to be sent out to the internet or kept within the MPLS cloud. This decision is based on the destination of the data and the associated security policies. In this way, MPLS allows CSV Family Health to create a private network within the larger public internet, providing secure and efficient communication between sites.

  • Disaster Recover

    • At CSV Family Health, we have implemented a Point-to-Point link as a backup solution in case of any unforeseen events that may occur at our Shafter Datacenter. This link is established between our Shafter and Corporate sites and serves as a redundant datacenter in case of a disaster at the primary site. The Point-to-Point link works by creating a dedicated and private communication channel between the two sites, allowing us to mirror data between them. In the event of a failure at the Shafter Datacenter, the Corporate site can take over and continue providing services to our users. This link acts as a safeguard against data loss and allows us to maintain business continuity in case of any disasters or unforeseen events.
